Split — a personal training instrument. Data: Intervals.icu (Garmin FR255) · scored by the daily coaching routine.

Methods & definitions

Day score

0–100, a probability-change reading: did the day move the odds of the 1:20 dream goal up (>60), hold them (~60), or lower them (<60)? Scored against 1:20 and independently against the committed 1:24–1:27 line. It rewards goal-fit, not effort — an easy run done genuinely easy scores high; intensity on an easy day scores low. See DAY_SCORE.md in the repo.

Raced-today odds

P(goal) = Φ((goal − predicted) / σ) with σ ≈ 2.5–3.5% of the prediction — the chance of hitting the goal if the race were run today at current fitness. No schedule risk, no forecasting: it rises only as fitness improves.

Pace & GAP

Pace comes from the device velocity stream (7 s smoothing for display; raw for computation). GAP (grade-adjusted pace) uses a Minetti-cost grade model with the per-activity level calibrated to Intervals.icu's Strava-model GAP, so splits sum consistently with the activity GAP. Km splits are computed on moving time (stops excluded), so split pace and split GAP share the same clock.

Session types & reps

Each run is classified from its velocity stream: sustained fast segments are detected as work, shaded on the pace chart, and tabulated as reps (intervals) or work blocks (tempo). Runs without structure read as easy / steady / long / threshold from intensity and duration. Watch autolaps are not shown — on steady runs they're just kilometer beeps. The Signals row is type-aware: easy/steady/long runs surface easy-purity (time in Z1–Z2), decoupling, EF and cadence; quality days surface work time/pace vs threshold and rep-to-rep fade.

Decoupling (Pw:Hr)

Split the moving time in half; EF₁/EF₂ = mean GAP speed ÷ mean HR per half; decoupling = (EF₁−EF₂)/EF₁. Positive = the same speed cost more heart rate late (aerobic drift). Intervals.icu's value is shown when it computes one; it's a clean base signal only on steady runs ≥ ~25 min — on progressions/workouts the negative split dominates, flagged accordingly.

Efficiency factor (EF)

Meters travelled per heartbeat at GAP speed (m/beat). Rising EF at the same HR = the base building.

Power

Native Garmin wrist running power. NP = 30 s rolling average → mean of 4th powers → ⁴√.

Cadence & stride

Device records per-leg rpm; shown as steps/min (×2). Stride = speed ÷ steps-per-second.

HR zones

Z1≤145 · Z2≤153 · Z3≤162 · Z4≤171 · Z5≤176 · Z6≤181 · Z7≤190 (LTHR 172, max 190). Zone times prefer Intervals.icu's computation, else derived from the HR stream.

Intensity buckets (volume chart)

Per-activity Intervals intensity (GAP speed ÷ threshold speed): easy <80 · steady 80–88 · threshold 88–96 · hard ≥96. Unrated short fragments count as easy.

Critical speed

CS/D′ fit by Intervals.icu on the 1-year pace curve. Short-duration-biased for low-volume athletes — read as a ceiling marker, not a threshold prescription.

Streams & sampling

1 s device streams; runs longer than ~25 min are stored at 2–3 s resolution (adaptive, analytically lossless for these metrics). Aggregates always come from Intervals.icu directly. GPS traces are never published.
